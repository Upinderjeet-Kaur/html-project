Top 5 Successful Women Entrepreneurs in India

Women entrepreneurs in India from all age groups and backgrounds are constantly smashing stereotypes and changing the business scene. These inspirational women have also forged their own paths to success, inspiring countless others in the process.

According to Forbes India, over 20% of MSMEs are owned by women entrepreneurs in India which amounts to 23.3% of the labor force.

Let us look at the list of 5 trailblazing women entrepreneurs in India that left no stone unturned.

  1. Kiran Mazumdar Shaw

    2. Company : Biocon Pharmaceuticals

    Kiran Mazumdar Shaw is the executive chairperson and founder of Biocon Limited and Biocon Biologics Limited, a biotechnology company based in Bangalore, India and the former chairperson of Indian Institute of Management, Bangalore.

      Achievements :

    • Othmer Gold Medal for outstanding contributions to the progress of science and chemistry.
    • She was on the Financial Times 2011 top 50 women in business list.
    • In 2019, she was listed as the 68th most powerful woman in the world by Forbes. She was named EY World Entrepreneur Of The Year 2020.

  2. Falguni Nayar

    3. Company : Nykaa

    Falguni Sanjay Nayar (born 19 February 1963) is an Indian billionaire businesswoman, who is the founder and CEO of the beauty and lifestyle retail company Nykaa, formally known as FSN E-Commerce Ventures which is an acronym of her own name.

      Achievements :

    • EY Entrepreneur of the Year Award 2022
    • Forbes World’s 100 Most Powerful Women and Bloomberg Global Annual 50
    • awarded the Isidoro Alvarez Lifetime Achievement Medal at the World Retail Congress in Barcelona, Spain for her outstanding contribution to the global retail industry.

  3. Vandana Luthra

    4. Company : VLCC

    Vandana Luthra is an Indian entrepreneur and the founder of VLCC Health Care Ltd, a beauty and wellness conglomerate represented in Asia, the GCC and Africa. She is also the chairperson of the Beauty & Wellness Sector Skill Council (B&WSSC), an initiative that provides training under the Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana schemes.
    She was appointed the first chairperson of the Beauty and Wellness Sector Skill Council in 2014.

      Achievements :

    • Padma Shri (India’s fourth highest civilian honor) in 2013 for her contribution to trade and industry.
    • The Asian Business Leaders Forum Trailblazer Award in 2012
    • The Enterprise Asia Women Entrepreneur of the Year Award in 2010

  4. Namita Thapar

    5. Company : Emcure Pharmaceuticals

    Namita Thapar is the CEO of Emcure Pharmaceuticals, an Indian multinational pharmaceutical company based in Pune, Maharashtra. She is also known as the Pharma Queen of India.

      Achievements :

    • Barclays Hurun Next Gen Leader Recognition
    • The Economic Times 40 under 40 Award
    • World Women Leadership Congress Super Achiever Award
    • The Economic Times 2017 Women Ahead List

  5. Vineeta Singh

    6. Company : Sugar Cosmetics

    Vineeta Singh is an Indian entrepreneur and CEO and co-founder of Sugar Cosmetics.

      Achievements :

    • World Economic Forum's Young Global Leadership list (2022)
    • W-Power Award by Forbes India (2021)..
    • BW Disrupt 40 Under 40 Award by Businessworld (2021).
    • Fortune's 40 Under 40 (2021)
    • Start-up of the year award by Entrepreneur Awards, Delhi (2019).
